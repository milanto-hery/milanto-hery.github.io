I am a Data Scientist and ML Researcher who bridges the gap between environmental raw data and actionable conservation intelligence. My mission is to develop precise, scalable models that transform complex data into actionable ecological insights.

The Approach

"I don’t just create AI models; I build complete systems that turn research into practical tools for the real world."

My fascination with ML and data science began during my BSc in Physics at the University of Antananarivo. This led me to do my MSc focused on time-series prediction of sunspot activity using neural networks, where I realized the immense potential of AI in tackling real-world problems.

Later, at the African Institute for Mathematical Sciences (AIMS) and Stellenbosch University, I refined my expertise in ML and Signal Processing, focusing specifically on how these technologies can be used to help monitor and protect our natural world.

Global Engagement

Bridging Theory & Field Application

I attended the Foundational Methods in Data Science in Rwanda, and subsequently participated in the International Conference on Learning Representations (ICLR 2023) conference.

I presented my research at the Vatican African Conference on Climate Resilience in Nairobi, and participated in the dialogue towards sustainable ecological solutions.

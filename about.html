About Me

My name is Milanto. I am a data scientist and machine learning researcher. I also have a background in applied mathematics, physics and computational modeling. My work focuses on creating methods based on data that can help us understand complex situations and make the right decisions.

I was interested in science and technology from a young age. I was curious about how natural systems work and how technology can be used to understand and protect them.

My love of nature and computers guided my academic path. I did my BSc in Physics at the the University of Antananarivo, where I earned a solid foundation in mathematics, physical modelling and analytical reasoning. While I was doing my MSc, I became more and more interested in machine learning and data-driven methods. I decided to focus my project on machine learning for time-series prediction, which was a turning point for me in my work with artificial intelligence. This experience led to my selection for the African Institute for Mathematical Sciences (AIMS), where I completed an intensive one-year programme in mathematical sciences and machine learning.

At AIMS, I studied advanced machine learning methods and did research on data-driven modelling for neurodegenerative diseases. I then went on to do research in machine learning for ecology at Stellenbosch University, using AI techniques to monitor the environment and protect wildlife. My academic journey has been shaped by one goal: developing accurate machine learning methods that can be used to solve real scientific and social problems.

Alongside my academic training, I have engaged in scientific communication through conferences, workshops, and special training programs. I have presented my work on multiple occasions through posters, oral presentations and technical talks in different countries.

About Me

I am Milanto, a data scientist and machine learning researcher with a strong foundation in applied mathematics, physics, and computational modeling. My work focuses on building intelligent systems that can extract meaningful information from complex data and support decision-making in real-world contexts.

Over the years, I have worked across the full data science and machine learning pipeline, from raw data acquisition and preprocessing to model development, evaluation, and deployment. I place particular emphasis on understanding the structure and limitations of data, designing robust representations, and developing models that are both effective and interpretable.

My research interests are strongly interdisciplinary. I am particularly motivated by applications of artificial intelligence to environmental monitoring, wildlife conservation, and ecological analysis. This includes work in bioacoustics, computer vision, and remote sensing, where large-scale data and automated analysis are essential.

My Work

My work is driven by the belief that machine learning systems should be grounded in a deep understanding of both the data and the domain in which they are applied. Rather than treating models as black boxes, I focus on building pipelines that combine signal processing, statistical reasoning, and modern deep learning techniques.

I have extensive experience working with high-dimensional data such as audio signals, spectrograms, images, and video. This includes designing preprocessing workflows, selecting appropriate representations, and evaluating model performance using meaningful metrics. I am particularly interested in scalable systems that can operate reliably under real-world constraints.

Beyond technical development, I value clear communication and reproducibility. I aim to produce work that is well-documented, transparent, and accessible to both technical and interdisciplinary collaborators.
